# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

UK consumers in the research and comparison phase of an electronics purchase — laptops, phones, monitors, tablets, desktop PCs. They arrive with a vague need ("I need a new laptop") or a specific anxiety ("I don't want to buy the wrong thing"). Typically aged 18–45, ranging from students to working professionals. They've probably already spent time on Amazon, Currys, or Reddit and left more confused than when they arrived.

## Product Purpose

DEN is a decision intelligence engine. It exists to replace the anxiety of electronics research with a single, confident recommendation. Success is a user who closes the tab feeling they've made the right call — not one who opens four more tabs to double-check.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, premium. The authority of Wirecutter with the craft discipline of Stripe. Never hype, never urgency, never noise. DEN is the expert friend who has already done the research — they just tell you the answer.

## Anti-references

- **Autopilot SEO content farms** — AI-generated review networks that exist to capture search traffic, not serve readers. Thin content, affiliate-first structure, no real opinion. DEN should look and feel like the antithesis of this.
- **Price comparison aggregators** (Idealo, PriceRunner) — table-heavy, ad-ridden, zero editorial hierarchy. Optimised for crawlers, not humans.
- **Retailer product pages** (Amazon, Currys) — transactional density, star ratings, competing CTAs, feature lists as filler.
- **Generic AI/SaaS aesthetic** — gradient text, glowing hero metrics, "Powered by AI" badges, cream backgrounds. Anything that signals "this was built in a weekend."

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns decoration** — every visual element must carry information or reduce anxiety. Decoration for its own sake undermines trust.
2. **Calm before confident** — the tone is never urgent or pushy. The recommendation should feel inevitable, not sold.
3. **Take a stance** — ranked outputs with clear winners. "It depends" is a failure state. Users came for an answer.
4. **Zero regret architecture** — every page should reduce post-purchase anxiety, not amplify choice paralysis. Fewer options, clearer reasoning.
5. **Editorial authority** — DEN has a voice and a point of view. It's not a neutral aggregator. The intelligence behind the rankings should be legible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard-navigable quiz and results flows. Sufficient contrast on all text against dark backgrounds. Focus indicators visible on interactive elements. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`.
